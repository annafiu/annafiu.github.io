the small office workflow: sketchup, autocad, and beyond

february 2025 7 min read workflow

How a small design studio uses lightweight cloud frameworks, structured SketchUp organization, and collaborative DWG workflows to punch far above their weight.

the file collision problem

When multiple designers actively access identical AutoCAD drawings concurrently, overlapping progress risks overriding previous updates. We mitigated this friction through a lightweight file-lock routine — a straightforward AutoLISP mechanism that instantly flags localized networks when a DWG is checked out.

"the best workflow optimization is the one your team will instinctively implement without friction."

The core philosophy: avoid over-engineering structural operations. Small-scale studios require agile, fast-moving practices that actively respect lean budgets and non-linear working cycles.

sketchup as the design hub

We treat SketchUp as the central spatial environment of our operations — utilizing it not merely for late-stage 3D visualization, but as the active playground for negotiating key programmatic layouts prior to detailing drawings in AutoCAD.
