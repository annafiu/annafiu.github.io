bamboo in modern architecture: a tectonic exploration

october 2024 6 min read material

Bamboo is no longer just a traditional or temporary fallback material. With proper structural treatment and specialized engineering, engineered bamboo acts as an exceptionally high-performing, carbon-negative alternative to timber and steel.

understanding material treatment

Raw bamboo contains natural starches that make it highly vulnerable to insect attacks. Immersing sections in a borax-boric acid solution leaches out these sugars, rendering the fibers durable for structural implementation. Modern engineered bamboo allows for precise load calculations previously impossible with natural tapered poles.

connection details

The core challenge of bamboo design lies in its joints. Because poles are hollow, direct nailing splits the grain. True craftsmanship combines fish-mouth joints, structural steel plates inside the cavities, and clean pin connections to cleanly distribute tensile loads.
